My Portfolio / Mission

My Name is Victor Edun. And I am on a mission To democratize programming.

I am on a mission to empower American students become competent computer programmers who can use technology to change the world. Coding can be challenging, like anything else. But anyone can learn coding. If you are speaking a human language, you already understand a bit of how computer language works.

Like all languages, computer languages have their own rules and syntax. Understand those rules, and you will be able to understand and read even the most complex code. Practice and apply those rules in creating programs, and you are on your way to becoming a programmer. Let me help you begin this journey today. Lt. Ola, The First Afronaut



The first Gumundan person to make an interplanetary travel was Lieutenant Ola, the leader of the Amazon Brigade and hero of war of Sousou. Two technologies made this two way trip possible: Hyperspace technology and the Gumundan Telomere therapy, a breakthrough that slowed human aging to a crawl. Lt. Ola’s feat is celebrated in songs, notably the ” Ola alagbara” or “Ola, the strong one” (This is a rough translation of the Yoruba language-the language of the Gumundans, spoken by the Yoruba tribe, a tribe that traces its history to the ancient Gumundans.)

Captain Nabi’s Interstellar Journey to the Andromeda Galaxy



Captain Nabi was already a decorated war hero and a celebrated pilot before she headed the first Interstellar trip to the Andromeda star system. While much of the known world was ruled by the barbaric Romans, the African nation of Gumunda was an advanced ancient civilization-one that used cloaking technologies to hide its existence. But as the centuries wore on, it became apparent that because the earth’s other nations would continue their warlike ways, the elders and the leaders of Gumunda decided on an audacious plan: A mass exodus from the Earth to a new home- way beyond the milky way galaxy.

And so Gumunda began a space program. Leading academics across the nation were able to create wormhole technologies that would enable the Gumundan Arks. But before this, there was a need to test the technologies. Captain Nabi and her crew were the first to make this interstellar travel. They traveled to the Andromeda galaxy and successfully set up a colony. A few decades later, they returned and the mass evacuation from Gumunda to planet Ade, in the Andromeda galaxy.

I was inspired to create these series of Images of this alternate reality because of the negativity and poor portrayal of Africa and people of African origins in the Media.This is my attempt to, if you will, balance the equation. And show the world an image of Africa not characterized by war, terrorism, disease and poverty

Princess Shade



Princess Sade, sole heir to the throne of King Ade, the King of Gumunda. But in her younger carefree days, the Princess loved high fashion. Her favorites? The golden “iro” and “boba”

She also loved her big afro and large golden earrings inlaid with rubies. The reign of princess Sade is often called ” The Golden Age of Gumunda” because it was during that period that Gumundan innovation exploded. Cures for disease like cancer was discovered, therapy to slow down old age was developed and Gumunda made its first interplanetary manned flight to Mars.

Gumundan Male Fashion Wears and looks



Like their female counterparts, Gumundan men also like their clothing bright with accents of gold and silver.They also like to wear their hair as big balls that we know today as the “afro.” Because of this, Gumundan men rarely ever cut their hair from childhood to old age. Instead they maintain them with natural shampoos derived from the gumundis isolatae a plant with shampoo properties that keeps the hair clean and shiny.

In big social events like fairs, national holidays or parties, it is not unusual to see these big, black, fluffy balls of Gumundan masculinity, extending as far as the eyes can see.
